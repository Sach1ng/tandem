[div] The idea
[h2] Most AI tools are a chat box.
[h2] Tandem flips that.
[p] A model wrapped in a chat window, locked to one vendor and one surface, that forgets you every
        session. Tandem is built the other way around.
[h3] The brain is yours — and it compounds
[p] Pip starts with a blank memory and builds context as you work, saving durable facts to a local
[code] memory/
[p] it reuses on every surface. Have a capability OS like PM OS? Pip mounts it
        as a rich starting point. Nothing is required to begin.
[h3] The engine is model-agnostic
[p] Every surface shells out to one shared adapter around
[code] cursor-agent
[p] . Switch from
[code] gpt-5
[code] sonnet-4
[code] auto
[p] with an env var — no new
        integration, no vendor lock-in.
[h3] The surfaces meet you where you are
[p] A Slack teammate, a floating desktop widget, and a browser extension — all calling the
[em] same
[p] engine against the
[em] same
[p] workspace. One coworker, not three bots.

[div] FAQ
[h2] Questions people actually ask
[p] The practical stuff — what you do, what Pip does, and where everything runs.
[button] So my team does… what, exactly?
[p] Ask in Slack, snip on desktop, or assign from Chrome — then steer or approve. Pip runs the Cursor agent against your workspace: reads memory, writes artifacts, logs decisions, and ships the outcome back on whichever surface you started from. You stay in the loop; Pip does the research, drafting, and file moves.
[button] Who actually runs the agent?
[p] You do — on your machine. Every surface shells out to
[code] cursor-agent
[p] with your repo as context. There is no Tandem cloud. Your models, your disk, your workspace.
[button] Is this a Cursor, Slack, or Google product?
[p] No. Tandem is an open-source personal project (MIT). It plugs into those surfaces but is not affiliated with any vendor or employer. Swap the CLI engine when you want — the surfaces and memory layer stay the same.
[button] Do I need PM OS or a skills library to start?
[p] No. Pip boots with blank
[code] memory/
[p] and builds context as you work. Mount PM OS (or any knowledge tree) if you want a rich starting point — totally optional.
[button] What about privacy and memory?
[p] Memory lives locally under your workspace — profile, activity log, and durable facts Pip reuses across Slack, desktop, and browser. No hosted brain, no cross-tenant data. You control what stays in the repo.
[button] Which models and tools work?
[p] Whatever the Cursor CLI supports — GPT, Claude, Gemini, Auto, and more. Set
[code] CURSOR_AGENT_MODEL
[p] or pass flags per call. From browser and desktop, Pip can also use WebSearch, shell, file writes, and MCP when you assign work with full autonomy.
